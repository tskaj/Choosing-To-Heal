Frequently Asked Questions

What is Psychotherapy?

Psychotherapy is a process where a trained professional, through a therapeutic relationship facilitates you to explore and understand your thoughts, feelings, and behaviors and work towards achieving your goals and bring positive changes in a safe, non-judgmental and collaborative environment.

Do I need a referral to see a psychotherapist?

You do not need a referral to see a psychotherapist. You can make an appointment with the psychotherapist on your own.

How do I book an appointment?

Please reach out to me if you have any questions or would like to schedule a 15 minutes free consultation to see if we are a good fit for each other by using this contact form. You can also book an appointment by clicking here.

Will my psychotherapy session be covered by OHIP?

Psychotherapy services are not covered by OHIP. You may have an insurance benefits package that may provide coverage for the service provided. Please contact your insurance provider to find out details of coverage for psychotherapy sessions before booking the first session.

How much does a psychotherapy session cost?

Fees for a 50-minute individual session: $160 CAD
Fees for a 50-minute couple/family session: $195 CAD
Fees for a 90-minute Accelerated Resolution Therapy session: $200 CAD

How do I pay for the therapy sessions?

Payments are accepted through cash, credit card, and e-transfer soon after the session.

Do you offer a sliding scale fee structure?

I do have limited spots to offer psychotherapy sessions on a sliding scale fee structure (reduced rate). Please let me know if finances are a concern, and I’d be happy to discuss a fee structure that works for you.

What is your cancellation policy?

We require 24 hours’ notice when you cancel an appointment. Appointments canceled within 24 hours or missed appointments will be subject to 50% of the session fees.

What to do in a crisis?

In case of immediate crisis, please call 911, go to the nearest emergency department, contact your family physician, or call a Distress Centre.
